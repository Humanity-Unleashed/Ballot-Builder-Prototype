Blueprint: Values Lead, Policies Stay Separate

The moral foundations profile is the shareable front door — novel self-discovery that feels like a personality test. Policy positions live in a separate layer with no causal claims between them. Every score level is described positively.

Two Problems Solved
1
"Low Care" feels like a moral judgment. Fixed: every score level gets a genuinely positive description. "Authority: 35" becomes "Self-directed — you prefer to question expectations and decide your own path."
2
"High Care = supports public healthcare" is editorializing. Fixed: values and positions live in separate screens. No arrows between them. Two people with identical values can have opposite policy preferences.
Two Separate Layers
Level 1 is the values profile — shareable, novel, non-political. Level 2 is the policy positions — private detail, no value attribution. Level 3 shows why we keep them separate.

Why?
Alex thinks Care means universal coverage — no one should fall through the cracks. Sam thinks Care means better quality — competition improves what patients actually get. Same value, different logic.

This is why we show your values and your positions side by side, not as cause and effect. Your values are the lens — your positions are the conclusion you reached through that lens.
