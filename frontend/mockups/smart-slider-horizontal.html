Design Principles

  • Center = Current Policy: The middle position (grey) always represents current real-world policy. Users can see where we are today and choose to move in either direction.
  • Even Positions: When there's an even number (4, 6, etc.), current policy still gets the grey color at its designated position.
  • Positive Pole Labels: Instead of partisan framing, each pole emphasizes its positive value proposition:
    • "Universal Access" vs "Personal Choice" (not "Gov't" vs "Private")
    • "Community Safety" vs "Individual Rights"
    • "Expanded Support" vs "Personal Initiative"
    • "Rapid Action" vs "Steady Progress"
  • Brief Explanations: Each position includes a short description (in lighter text) highlighting the positive aspect of that particular stance.
